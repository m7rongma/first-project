Every beginner’s coding journey in frontend starts with these two basic building blocks and you need to be creative when it comes to designing a beautiful application

Initially, beginners enjoy making buttons, adding the links, adding images, working with layout and a lot of cool stuff in web designing but when it comes to making a project using only HTML and CSS they get stuck and confuse about what they should make to practice all these stuff. HTML headings are titles or subtitles that you want to display on a webpage.

Notea: Browsers automatically add some white space (a margin) before and after a heading.

Bigger Headings

Eah HTML Heading has a dfault size. However, you can specify the size for any heading eith the style attribute, using the CSS font-size property.

HTML Paragraphs

The HTML p element defines a paragraph.

A paragraph always starts on a new line, and browsers automatically add some white space (a margin) before and after a paragraph.

HTML Horizontal Rules

The hr tag defines a thematic break in an HTML page, and is most ofen displayed as a horizontal rule.

The hr element is used to separate content (or define a change) in an HTML page.

A Story of a striggling web developer

John was always interested in computers and technology from a young age. He would spend hours tinkering with old computers and software, trying to figure out how they worked. When he finished high school, he decided to pursue his passion and enrolled in a computer science program at a local university.

After graduating, John started his career as a web developer, eager to put his skills to the test. He landed a job at a small web development agency, where he worked long hours, often staying late into the night to complete projects on time.

Despite his hard work, John found it difficult to keep up with the rapidly changing technology landscape. He struggled to learn new programming languages and frameworks, and his colleagues seemed to be advancing much faster than he was.

John also struggled with the business side of things. He found it difficult to communicate effectively with clients and was often unsure of how to price his services or negotiate contracts.

As time went on, John became increasingly frustrated with his lack of progress. He felt like he was stuck in a rut and wasn't sure how to move forward.

But John refused to give up. He spent countless hours studying and practicing new programming languages and frameworks, determined to improve his skills. He also took courses in business and communication, learning how to better interact with clients and negotiate contracts.

Slowly but surely, John's hard work paid off. He started to feel more confident in his abilities and was able to take on more challenging projects. He even started getting positive feedback from clients, who appreciated his dedication and attention to detail.

Today, John is still working as a web developer, but he's come a long way since those early days. He's now a respected member of the web development community, and he's even started his own consulting business, helping other struggling developers find their way in the industry. Through hard work and perseverance, John was able to turn his passion for technology into a successful career.
